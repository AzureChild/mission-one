Introduction

If the descriptions get short and trail off it is because I am mainly just trying to fill this area with text and images to test and demonstrate my navigation sidebar. That said, Mekk-Knights are an archetype from the Yu-Gi-Oh Trading Card Game. In today's meta they are definitely overlooked due to power creep but they still retain some powerful and unique abilities that allow them to compete at a rogue level and can even surprise and defeat stronger archetypes through shock value and unfamiliarity.


Their archetypal effect gives inherent summoning from hand and their access to an in-engine, searchable Emergency Teleport gives them a limited ability to swarm the field with high attack monsters. This is bolstered with some strong, in-engine backrow support in cards like 'World Legacy's Secret' and 'World Legacy Key'. Combined with their column mechanics the result is a fun and unique mid-range strategy that has multiple options for Normal Summoning and playing Second.

Deckbuilding
Purple Nightfall x3
The backbone of any Mekk-Knight strategy and arguably our best starter. It searches the rest of our Mekk-Knights to start combos, in a pinch it can banish another Mekk-Knight to protect it and it returns in every standby phase to a column of our choice to continue gaining advantage or can be used in conjunction with our backrow to ensure columns are locked down during our turn if the opponent still has board presence. When searching Blue Sky it also self-clears itself from the column to continue plays. In some circumstances it can also be used for Rank 8 plays with another Nightfall or with Eclipse. Highly recommended to play at 3 copies.
Blue Sky x2-3
Nightfall's best friend and a common search target but also a perfectly fine starter card. Blue Sky can search Nightfall when needed or get any other name (usually Girsu) for extension. The downsides are that it cannot clear its own column and requires an opponents card to get searches. This card is one of the best reasons to choose to go second with a Mekk-Knight Strategy. 2 copies is fine as it is a weaker opener - especially for mixed engines but 3 will help maintain consistency.
Girsu, the Orcust Mekk-Knight x1-2
Arguably our best Normal Summon, he is unfortunately still stuck in semi-limited prison. Despite that, you may want to keep it at 1 or 2 copies anyway. Girsu does a lot - sending World Legacy cards to enable Keymaster lines and acting as his own extender whilst setting up columns for other Mekk-Knights to summon into. With a bit of deckbuilding he can even operate as a one card Zeus. However outside of this opening just Girsu is not exactly full combo, which is why it is best searched off of Nightfall. The fact he is a normal summonable Mekk-Knight also cannot be overlooked as sometimes column space is at a premium. Any hand containing 2 of these 3 above cards is one of the strongest in-engine openers we have and typically will end on I.P Masquerena plus Keymaster.
